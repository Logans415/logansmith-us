Hello, world (again)

I rebuilt this site from scratch. The old version was a tired template on a paid builder — fine, but not mine, and not something I'd actually want to point a recruiter or a curious peer at. So I tore it down and rebuilt it as a fast, static, version-controlled site I fully control. (If you're curious how the move went, that's its own post.)

This blog is the part I'm most excited about. I learn best by writing things down, and the security field is full of stuff that's poorly documented or buried behind jargon. So I'm going to write the posts I wish I'd found.

What to expect here

  • Security notes — practical defensive security, fundamentals done well, and lessons from breaking things on purpose.
  • Homelab — what I'm running, what broke, and what I learned hosting my own services.
  • Automation — small scripts and agents that remove human error and give me time back.
  • Occasional rabbit holes — AI/ML, web tinkering, and whatever has my attention.

No fixed schedule, no fluff — just things I found worth writing down. If something here helps you, or you want to compare notes, reach out. Thanks for stopping by.
